# Grammar Frameworks (teach from these for consistency)

## Gender traps (g01) — memorize the exceptions
- Most `-o` = masculine, most `-a` = feminine. **Reliable feminine endings:** `-ción/-sión, -dad/-tad, -tud, -umbre`.
- **Masculine despite `-a`:** el problema, el tema, el sistema, el programa, el día, el mapa, el clima, el idioma, el sofá.
- **Feminine despite `-o`:** la mano, la foto, la moto, la radio.
- **`-ma`/`-pa`** from Greek are usually masculine (el problema, el mapa). `el agua` is feminine but takes `el` (stressed a-).
- Adjective agrees in gender AND number: `el carro rojo / los carros rojos / la casa roja / las casas rojas`.

## Preterite vs. imperfect (g09) — the decision framework
**Core metaphor: photo vs. movie.** Preterite = a snapshot, a completed event with edges ("it happened, done").
Imperfect = the rolling film, the background, no edges ("it was going on").

**Use IMPERFECT for:** (1) habits / repeated past ("used to, would"), (2) descriptions & background — weather,
age, time, feelings, physical/mental states, (3) an ongoing action that gets interrupted, (4) telling time/date in the past.

**Use PRETERITE for:** (1) a single completed action, (2) a sequence of events ("first… then…"), (3) an action
with a clear start or end, (4) a sudden reaction.

**The interruption rule:** the ongoing thing = imperfect; the thing that cuts in = preterite —
*"Comía cuando sonó el teléfono."*

**Meaning-shift verbs (high value):** sabía *knew* → supe *found out* · conocía *knew (someone)* → conocí *met* ·
quería *wanted* → quiso *tried* / no quiso *refused* · podía *was able* → pudo *managed to*.

## WEIRDO — present subjunctive triggers (g21)
**W**ishes (*Quiero que vengas*) · **E**motion (*Me alegra que estés*) · **I**mpersonal expressions (*Es importante que estudies*) ·
**R**ecommendations/requests (*Te recomiendo que descanses*) · **D**oubt/denial (*No creo que sea verdad*) · **O**jalá (*Ojalá llueva*).
Plus **future time clauses** (*cuando, hasta que, antes de que, en cuanto* + subjunctive when the event hasn't happened yet).
**Formation:** yo present indicative → drop `-o` → add opposite vowel (`-AR`→`e`, `-ER/-IR`→`a`). Irregulars: ser→sea, ir→vaya, haber→haya, estar→esté, saber→sepa, dar→dé.

## Por vs para (g18)
- **PARA** = destination, purpose, recipient, deadline: *para Madrid, para estudiar, para ti, para el lunes.*
- **POR** = cause/reason, exchange, duration, "through/by/along", means: *por la lluvia, gracias por, por dos horas, por el parque, por teléfono.*
